"Design like you're absolutely right, then optimize like you're wrong from the start" - Jared Spool


I taught myself Photoshop at the age of 12 to create a gift for my brother's 16th birthday—a photo where I superimposed his face onto a scene from The Godfather. Armed with YouTube tutorials and a trusty trackpad, I tinkered for days, perfecting every pixel. I absolutely loved it. Seeing people's reactions and receiving their feedback was the best part. I constantly refined my work until it was as close to perfect as possible. Soon, I became the go-to person for friends and family needing design magic.

Choosing to study design at OCAD was an easy decision for me. I loved art school because I got to collaborate with others, present my ideas, and work together to create something that truly resonated with people. The creative process is where I really shine.

Transitioning into marketing, I used my design skills to enhance brand identities through design systems and guidelines and became the "brand police" wherever I went. I also embraced data-driven decision-making, enjoying the comprehensive view of seeing projects through from concept to measurable outcomes.

Through my marketing work, I was exposed to web and mobile design and I fell in love with its technicality and creativity. Being a lifelong learner who loves school, I decided to further my education at UofT for UX/UI Design, and I was hooked!

Now, I do a lot of freelance work and currently work at Walmart Ecommerce, where I get to apply my design and research skills daily.

Thank you for visiting!
