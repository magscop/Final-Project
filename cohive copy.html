BACKGROUND

002

Background

Information

Have you ever struggled to find cool places in a new city? How successful were you? How long did it take you to decide where to go? Was it easy to get there?

After researching travel articles post-COVID-19, I noticed a flood of headlines filled with people eager to travel and an overwhelming array of recommendations and suggestions on what to do.

When you type "what is the best way to travel and explore new cities" into Google, the first suggestion to show up is "talk to locals.” Locals provide the best insights into hidden gems, top recommendations, local transit, and the most authentic or trendy restaurants. They can help you travel on a budget by showing their top day-to-day places or the best high-end places they go for special occasions.

By connecting users with locals, we can make it easier for them to experience authentic culture and make the most out of their trip, which in a post-COVID-19 world is something that is yearned for. This insight inspired the idea for “Local”, a platform where real locals offer trustworthy recommendations.

Empathize

I conducted a competitive and feature analysis of travel companion and booking platforms in the market. Then, I surveyed and interviewed people currently planning or thinking of booking a trip to gain insight into their motivation and pain points.

ANALYSIS

002

Research

Findings

To get a baseline understanding of users wants and needs surrounding travelling, I conducted 5 semi-structured interviews. Additionally, 10 individuals completed a survey. Here are the quantitative and qualitative findings.

92%

of respondants find planning activities for a trip stressfull. Many of them expressed that alot of time goes into planning a trip. One of them mentioned that they want to make the most out of visiting a new place and having a detailed itinerary can help them achieve this.

84%

of respondants prefer travelling to a destination where they know a local. Many of the respondants talked about their best trips being when they knew somebody who lived in the destintaion they were visiting.

79%

of respondants want to experience a destinations authentic culture. Many stated that they often plan their trip based on experiecing a destinations local culture. One stated "When I am on a trip, I want to "do as the Romans do"".

User Research

User Research

User research was essential in developing the CoHive app, as it provided insights into the challenges individuals face in creative fields when building portfolios or securing jobs. I conducted four interviews with professionals from acting, playwriting, music, and advertising backgrounds. These interviews informed a survey completed by 14 respondents. Insights regarding project management, networking, confidentiality, and copyright emerged from this research. These findings helped identify the primary features of the app while also revealing the definition of success for a creative development app.

Key Insights

Insights for app development

By condensing all user feedback into key points, which were subsequently tested through a Google survey, we identified the primary challenges creatives encounter when entering the job market. This process unveiled the significant pain points and highlighted how an application like CoHive could effectively address these issues, and ultimately become a go-to platform for users. Key features were unvailed through this process.

Competitive Analysis: Allows you to understand your competitors' strengths and weaknesses and to find a gap in the market.

Competitive Analysis

Drawing upon insights gathered from user interviews and an extensive competitive analysis, I thoroughly explored platforms like Behance, Dribbble, Facebook, Hinge, LinkedIn, and Reddit to find interface inspiration across onboarding, profile creation, and projects/job stages. My goal was to create an application that seamlessly resonates with users, evoking a sense of familiarity. By examining apps tailored for inspiration, networking, group collaboration, and professional development, I aimed to identify essential features vital for CoHive, with the aim of exceeding existing applications in functionality and user experience.

Competitor Product Analysis: Helps you identify where there are gaps to fill, and the opportunities you can take advantage of.

Sketches & Prototyping

Ideation & Development

The design and features of the app were developed systematically. A storyboard helped establish the lead up to downloading the app and the value proposition chart helped prioritize features based on user needs. Then, sketches were created for each screen, refining them based on usability principles and feedback. This method ensured an intuitive and effective user experience.

Low-Fidelity Prototyping

Building on my initial sketches and insights gained from the value proposition chart and a feature analysis, I developed the low-fidelity prototype that followed the onboarding, profile creation, project posting and management and the overall vision of what the home feed would like. Building out the design was a very lengthy process that required many revisions before I finalized on a version I would user test with.

Low-Fidelity Prototype: Allows us to quickly and inexpensively test ideas, so we can validate our hypotheses and improve our solutions.

Mid & High-Fidelity Prototyping

User Testing Results & Redesign

Six user tests were performed on both low-fidelity and mid-fidelity prototypes, in conjunction with four heat maps of key content screens. This process yielded deeper insights into the app's usability. Feedback primarily centred on the overall design of the application, with users also expressing a desire for greater functionality expansion.



Mid-Fidelity Redesign

The design of the mid-fidelity prototype evolved through iterative processes following user tests conducted on tasks such as onboarding, profile creation, project posting, and home feed exploration. Subsequent to these tests and further exploration of the app's features, the UI style tile was utilized to guide the prototype's design. Additional iterations were made based on findings from user testing and continued exploration of the app's functionalities.


High-Fidelity Redesign

In tailoring the high-fidelity redesign to Cohive users, we conducted targeted user research and collected feedback to understand their needs. We simplified complex features based on user frustrations and prioritized enhancements like real-time collaboration tools, guided by user input. Through iterative design and active user involvement, we created a final product that aligns closely with Cohive users' expectations, delivering a tailored and intuitive experience.
