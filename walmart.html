WALMART

OVERVIEW

001

Project

Overview

I redesigned the commercial restaurant equipment page to deliver a user-centered, intuitive shopping experience. By improving product discoverability and streamlining navigation, I boosted conversions by 33% (YoY), reduced the bounce rate by 15%(YoY), and increased click-through rates on filtered pages by 22%(YoY).

The Internal Process

The redesign project started with a deep dive into the existing search functionality and user journey. Surprisingly, despite having all the right products, Walmart didn’t offer much of an experience. I was also taken aback to discover that Walmart was even carrying commercial restaurant equipment. The task at hand was clear: create an engaging and intuitive experience for users, making it easier for them to find what they needed while highlighting the value of this unexpected category.

By stepping into the customer's shoes, I pinpointed key pain points in navigation and product discovery. This gave me a clear understanding of how users were interacting with the site, driving the need for a more intuitive and seamless experience.

To validate these insights, I conducted a competitive analysis, looking at top eCommerce players both within the commercial restaurant equipment space and in other industries.

User tests and heat maps revealed how users were shopping this specific category and where they struggled most. These insights pinpointed key friction points and helped me focus the redesign on improving navigation, simplifying filtering, and highlighting top-searched products to better align with user behavior.

Execution

After validating the wireframes internally and ensuring they aligned with user needs, I handed them off to the development team, accompanied by detailed annotations and documentation. During the execution phase, I focused on ensuring the experience was implemented exactly as designed, thoroughly testing it both before and after launch to identify any errors. I also made sure the site maintained its performance standards throughout the process.

After launch, I conducted A/B testing to refine the user experience further. By comparing the new layout’s performance against the old version, I gathered data on key metrics like bounce rate, conversion rates, and click-through rates. The results were clear:

* 15% YoY decrease in bounce rate
* 33% YoY increase in conversions
* 22% YoY rise in click-through rates on filtered pages

By combining competitive insights with user-centred design principles, I not only enhanced the shopping experience but also positioned our platform as a competitor.

OUTCOME

002

New

Features

I revamped the page with improved navigation, search, filtering, personalized recommendations, and a clear layout, driving better user engagement and higher conversions. Check out the new key features below:

Interactive "Circle Chips"

Introduced visual filters to help users refine searches by product type, brand, and related items, enhancing browsing and upselling opportunities.


Reorganized Site Hierarchy

Redesigned both the backend and frontend to create a logical structure. On the frontend, I implemented "main circles" to display key categories alongside top-searched subcategories, making it easier for users to navigate and find what they need. This increased click-through rates and improved user jouneys.


High-Impact Product Cards for Enhanced Discovery

I designed product cards that highlighted high-volume, frequently searched commercial restaurant equipment to align with user needs and browsing behaviours. Each card featured visually engaging images that showcased the product in a real-world context, helping users better understand its application and suitability.

Within the card design, I integrated the actual high-volume searched products, ensuring users could quickly identify popular and relevant items. This approach not only created a more personalized shopping experience but also encouraged exploration and boosted engagement by connecting users with the products they were most likely to search for or need.

Left-Hand Navigation for Streamlined Browsing

To enhance usability and make the browsing experience more intuitive, I introduced a left-hand navigation menu that allowed users to easily visualize and access all subcategories. This menu provided a clear, hierarchical view of product categories, enabling users to navigate seamlessly without losing context.

Brand Tiles Connecting Walmart to Trusted Suppliers

I implemented brand tiles that showcased the most well-known products from top commercial restaurant equipment brands. These tiles were designed to provide users with a clear connection to trusted names in the industry, making it easy to explore brand-specific offerings.

By tying Walmart to these reputable brands, the tiles reinforced Walmart’s position as a reliable supplier for commercial restaurant equipment while helping users discover popular products more efficiently. This feature built trust and aligned Walmart’s offerings with the expectations of professionals in the industry.


Traffic Drivers

To drive traffic back to the commercial restaurant equipment page, I designed a sleek, adaptable skinny banner that could be seamlessly integrated across other related categories. This banner featured concise messaging, engaging visuals, and a clear call-to-action, encouraging users to explore the commercial restaurant equipment offerings.
