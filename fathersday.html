FATHER'S DAY WALMART

OVERVIEW

001

Project

Overview

Walmart’s Father’s Day Digital Gifting Experience was redesigned to help users easily find the perfect gift, reducing decision fatigue and making the process intuitive. The experience highlighted gifts based on popular interests among fathers, creating a personalized and relevant shopping journey.

Using research, testing, and competitor analysis, we improved product discoverability, simplified navigation, and streamlined the Add-to-Cart process. The design also reinforced Walmart’s ‘Everyday Low Prices’ (EDLP) initiative, building trust by consistently communicating value.

Problem

The project required designing a digital experience that helped users find the perfect Father’s Day gift while reducing decision fatigue and making the selection process intuitive and enjoyable. A key challenge was providing a personalized experience by understanding popular interests and preferences among fathers, ensuring that gift suggestions felt relevant and thoughtful.

Walmart faced two broader challenges: the shift to the ‘Everyday Low Prices’ (EDLP) initiative, which required building trust in consistently low prices rather than relying on temporary discounts, and the vast product selection on the platform, which risked overwhelming users.

From a design perspective, an additional challenge was collaborating with tech teams and merchandisers to fully understand product offerings and technical constraints, ensuring that the experience was both feasible and aligned with inventory realities.

Final Designs &

Solutions

Over the course of 12 weeks, I designed a Father’s Day digital gifting experience that balanced technical constraints and aligned with merchandisers’ product offerings. The experience was competitive within the marketplace, trend-driven, and relevant to customers

Father's Day Design Highlights

Streamlined Product Offerings

To simplify the shopping experience, we featured tiles front and center based on popular interests among Father’s Day shoppers. Each tile led to dedicated collections with curated products submitted by merchandisers and further refined by myself. These tiles rotated dynamically based on trends and competitor offerings, ensuring that the experience remained relevant, timely, and engaging while guiding users to the most appealing gift options.

Father's Day Design Highlights

Price Perception

A large portion of the experience was designed around price perception, showcasing Walmart’s core strength: delivering consistent value. By clearly communicating the ‘Everyday Low Prices’ (EDLP) message throughout the shopping journey, we reinforced trust with customers and highlighted that Walmart provides high-quality gifts at affordable prices. Strategic placement of prices, combined with curated collections and trend-driven tiles, ensured customers understood the value they were getting, encouraging confidence and driving conversions.

Father's Day Design Highlights

Personalized Gift Guidance

To help users quickly find the right gift, I worked closely with the tech team on the personalized recommendation system, helping define rules and scenarios that aligned with trending products and category-specific items. When a user selected a specific category, top products and other relevant categories appeared to guide them toward the most suitable options. This feature combined curated collections, trend insights, and user behavior data to reduce decision fatigue and make the gift-selection process faster and more enjoyable, enhancing engagement and overall user experience.

Father's Day Design Highlights

Responsive & Mobile-First Design

With most users engaging through Walmart’s mobile app and mobile web, we prioritized a mobile-first design approach. The experience was carefully optimized across both platforms to ensure shoppers could easily browse, filter, and add products to their cart from any device. I focused on asset sizing, touch target spacing, and content hierarchy to maintain clarity and usability on smaller screens. This consistency between app and web created a seamless shopping experience that met users where they are—on the go—while driving stronger engagement and conversion rates.
